Process of Coloring a Polyurethane casted piece

In misc.

tags: plastic

In this post, my idea is to document the process of coloring a polyurethane casted piece. After successfully achieving to create a 3d piece, I created a mold to cast multiple pieces. The casted pieces are always white due to the nature of the polyurethane.

In the image, we can see the preliminary results. We tried to cast a single face mold replicating a LEGO piece. We used several techniques:

  1. Polyurethane with no color (white)
  2. Epoxy with red (for epoxy) colorant (red)
  3. Acrylic painting with polyurethane (blue)
  4. Drawing ink with polyurethane (black)

With a first look, we can see that we faced some problems with the polyurethane + something. It looks like the mixture foam up -- as I could see in some youtube videos [REFERENCE] this happens when polyurethane interacts with water.

Polyurethane with no color and the epoxy resin + pigment showed better results.
