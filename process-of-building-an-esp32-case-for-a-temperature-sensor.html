This is a bitacora of my process of building an esp32 case for a temperature sensor.

Iteration #1

Aim: Make a case compatible with esp32-wroom-32. We expect to have a case with its lid. It should close and it should have space for the soldered pins.

  • Print: Iteration was a success: we can insert the esp32 inside.
  • Points to improve: (1) Missing USB hole; (2) Missing 4 cylinders to keep the board in place

Iteration #2

In the second iteration, I would like to have a hole for the DHT11. The DHT11 will have half of its body outside of the case. 4mm are needed inside the case to keep the pins. It is going to be glued with plastic pistol

  • Things to achieve
  • Add DHT11 hole (16x13mm) distance to the side half-half; distance to top 1cm;
  • Holes for the leds (23mm from the top, 7mm from left, 9mm from right).
  • Add a hole in the lateral structure to connect the USB

  • Add 4 supports to keep the board in the same position

    • validate the coordinates

  • Other defects

  • I do not know how to obtain a smoother surface

  • The good thing is that I can iterate with older cases.

  • Defects the DHT11 does not fit in the whole, we are missing 1mm and 1mm (doing a v2.5)
